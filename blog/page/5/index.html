Ortho Done!

I ended yesterday with a bang. There were only two surgeries scheduled for yesterday. First one was 4 hours long (which is kind of long in ortho land). It was around 1pm and we only had one more to do. I thought to myself, “Hurray! I get to go home early!” Big mistake.

The next case was … large woman needing a knee fusion, a surgery that’s hardly done anymore. I spent the next 7 hours on my feet holding up her legs or holding retractors while they tried to figure out what to do with her. I thought my feet were going to die. Today I think I have to rest my feet as much as possible…

Snowed Again…

Our routine weekly snowstorm hit again yesterday. The roads were icy and SUVs were overturned on the side of the road by careless drivers. I find it interesting that SUVs are so popular here when they have a higher center of gravity and are more prone to accidents, not just from the overconfidence of the drivers but from the physical characteristics of an SUV itself. The higher clearance of an SUV is great when you’re going up an incline or over rocks, but when it comes to frictionless physics, SUVs are definitely at a disadvantage. Not to mention that they add to the energy crisis…
