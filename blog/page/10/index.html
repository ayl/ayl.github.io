Ode to 86b

Oct 25th, 2006

and entered did we into his room,
that darkened place of many tears
where sadness ruled its futile reign.
entered we the flock of white,
the bringers of hope forlorn

what greatness that once held dear
onto this lifeless husk that lay
whose five adopted sons who came
and silently held his limp hand,
whispering words of love and tears

and how we stood there by his side
knowing not if he heard his name
pressing his nails and shining lights
twisting his head and pinching his skin
the absent signs of life.

medicine, though thou art be pure,
to where has gone our shame?
