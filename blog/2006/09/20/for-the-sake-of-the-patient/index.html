For the Sake of the Patient...

After my resident went into hiding, I, the lonely med student, was left to my own devices and decided to continue the on-going chess tournament with one of the psych patients. About an hour and a half later, I emerged victorious with a 7-5 lead, overall that makes us 10-7. What’s surprising about this patient is that he suffered head trauma, and the residents believed that he had cognitive impairment. After explaining to them that he doesn’t suffer from any discernable impairment, I turned to the patient and asked where he learned to play chess like that. He replied “the penitentiary.”
