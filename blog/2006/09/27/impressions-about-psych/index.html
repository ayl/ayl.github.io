Impressions About Psych...

Sep 27th, 2006

Now that I’m more than halfway through psych and have suffered through a few call nights. I feel that now would be a good time to re-evaluate what I think about psych. Usually what happens with each rotation that I’ve been is that I’ll be absolutely miserable for the first few weeks and then grow to like the rotation more and more as the time goes on.

Psych itself has been an interesting experience in that the first few weeks I didn’t feel like I was helping anybody at all, but the patients were fascinating in how different they were. Now that I’ve got a little more firm grasp of the psych wards I’ve become to appreciate it more. I realized that I like the subtle ways of phrasing sentances so that patients will help you help them, and the uniquness of the field of psychiatry in that this is probably the only field of medicine where you can’t trust the patients nor do some of the patients want to be helped. Ironically, psych seems to be closest to pediatrics in similarity: you can’t trust what the kids tell you, and not all the kids want to get better (especially if it involves a shot).
